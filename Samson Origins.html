BELGRADO

SAMSON ORIGINS

Premise

Before he became a father and figure of quiet power in Belgrado, Samson Yvette was a boy clawing his way out of the dust of Cameroon. After leaving behind his childhood friend Aviwe, Samson walks alone into a world that will destroy, remake, and redefine him.

4-Chapter Structure

  • Chapter 1: "The Goat"
    Theme: Childhood, loyalty, and the slow burning of innocence.
    Set in 1982 Cameroon, Samson (age 12) lives with his mother Aminatou, abusive stepfather Mbarga, and sibling Ndolo in harsh poverty. He works the mines after school, faces bullying and police corruption, and forms a deep bond with his best friend Aviwe. After a violent family incident, Samson chooses to leave Cameroon alone, telling Aviwe to "stay in the light," marking the death of boyhood and the birth of survival instinct.

  • Chapter 2: "The Scorcher"
    Theme: Trauma, violence, and transformation.
    Ages 13–15. Alone, Samson witnesses executions, joins JUA KALI activity, and survives a massacre where his mother is killed. Betrayal leads to loss and pain, and he ultimately joins the national military hungry for control over his life.

  • Chapter 3: "The Soldier"
    Theme: Power, love, betrayal.
    At 16, Samson rises quickly in the military, becoming cold and distant. He falls into a doomed relationship with a married woman who helps him reclaim his humanity, but he must ultimately choose between duty and love, walking away with a heavy heart.

  • Chapter 4: "The Split Path"
    Theme: Fate, legacy, and the cost of becoming something bigger.
    Nearing the end of his service, Samson meets Carmela, a fiery lawyer who challenges him. Drawn into the YMC and early LYRA frameworks, Samson stands alone at the film’s end—no longer the soldier, but a symbol born.

Core Characters

  • Aviwe: Samson’s childhood friend, symbolizing hope and innocence, appears only in Chapter 1.

  • Mbarga: The abusive stepfather, representing toxic masculinity, gone by Chapter 2.

  • Aminatou: Samson’s mother, a symbol of grace amid suffering; her death is a major turning point.

  • The Married Woman: Represents intimacy and healing in Chapter 3, but remains unnamed and left behind.

  • Chuma: A key figure tied to Samson’s journey and the unfolding legacy.
